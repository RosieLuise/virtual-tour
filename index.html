Explore the DANK Haus's 4th floor history exhibition Lost German Chicago, beginning with The Journey, which has artifacts from ship voyages from Germany to the U.S. From there, move on to "Clashes," "Club Life," "Beers & Brats," and more.

Highlights from the Collection

Olympia Typewriter

Circa 1930
This "Olympia Model 7" typewriter (German: Schreibmaschine) was manufactured in 1930 by Europa Schreibmaschinen AG. Click below to see what's on the back!

Beer Stein

Circa 1949 – 1990
"The person who drinks beer sleeps well. Whoever sleeps well does not sin." This cup's rhyming message encourages beer drinking as a healthy and moral pastime. Click to see the translation.

Drum

1868
German immigrants created a thriving musical culture in Chicago. This Civi War era drum was used by the Senefelder Liederkranz – the Senefelder choir.

Passport & Luggage Tag

1950s
Alwine Juretzko was born in Bottrop in 1919. Her Reisepass – passport – allowed her to come to New York City in 1954. "ORD," of course, stands for O'Hare Airport. It was newly opened at the time of Alwine's arrival.

Menu

Circa 1930s
The Old Heidelberg Inn was at Randolph and State in downtown Chicago. The Eitel brothers immigrated from Stuttgart, Germany. Click below to see an exterior view of the restaurant.

Neon Sign

Circa 2000
Pabst may have been a Milwaukee brewery, but the Chicago market was key to its success. Many bars were "tied houses," meaning they could only sell one brand of beer. Click to learn more about Chicagoans' thirst for Schlitz.
